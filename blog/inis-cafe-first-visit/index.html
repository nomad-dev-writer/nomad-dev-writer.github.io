INIS, first visit

Posted on 2024-09-09

INIS, London [website] [map]

Brioche bun with black pudding

  • Nice and soft brioche texture, toasted just about right
  • Fragrant and thick black pudding
  • Fresh spinach, but barely tastable as there were only two (giant) leaves
  • No sauce, but they provided ketchup, mayonnaise or brown sauce on-demand

The combination gave very balanced and subtle flavour, although I personally wished a little more kick to it. They probably minimised the amount of secondary ingredients to spotlight their black pudding, but it didn't stand out as much due to its mildness. Some may feel this bland, but its gentle and comfortable savour definitely is an excellent pairing with a decent cup of coffee.

Brown bread

Didn't manage to take photo of this, but it's a classic Irish brown bread with mild sweetness very extra soft texture. It is completed with nice touch of butter.

Coffee

They use beans that are roasted in the neighbour, at BAD coffee. It seemed to be medium-dark roast. It goes fairly well with the breads.

Overall

Their breads – both the bun and brown bread – were quite enjoyable with exceptional texture, toasted to perfection. I could clearly see their meticulousness in selecting Irish ingredients. I'm not yet convinced if their selection of ingredients are really standing out as a complete dish, but would definitely give another try. Pricewise, it seems to be aiming towards higher-end coffee and brunch place. Definitely not a place I would be able to afford to go every Saturday morning.
